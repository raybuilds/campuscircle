Safety & Trust

Use this page when something feels wrong — safety issues, harassment, health concerns, or emergencies. These are official resources and clear steps, not advice blogs.

Anti-Ragging Helpline

Use this if you are facing ragging, intimidation, or harassment in hostels, colleges, or PGs.

Action

Call: 1800-180-5522
Email: helpline@antiragging.in

What to say

  • Mention your college / hostel / PG name
  • Describe what happened and when
  • Say clearly if you feel unsafe right now

What happens next

  • Your complaint is officially recorded
  • Concerned authorities are informed
  • You may receive a follow-up call

Women’s Safety Helpline

Use this for harassment, stalking, threats, or situations where you feel unsafe.

Action

Call: 181 or 1091

What to say

  • Share your current location clearly
  • Explain what is happening right now
  • Mention if immediate help is required

What happens next

  • You are connected to local support
  • Immediate assistance is arranged if needed
  • Follow-up support may be offered

Mental Health Support (Tele-MANAS)

Use this if you are feeling overwhelmed, anxious, panicked, or emotionally exhausted.

Action

Call: 14416 or 1-800-891-4416

What to say

  • Say how you are feeling right now
  • Explain what is bothering you
  • Ask for immediate emotional support if needed

What happens next

  • You speak to a trained mental-health professional
  • They help calm the situation
  • You may be guided to further resources

Theft or Lost Property

Use this if your belongings are stolen or missing inside hostel, PG, or nearby areas.

Action

Contact local security / warden first.
For emergencies, call 112.

What to do

  • Note where and when the item was last seen
  • Inform hostel/PG authorities immediately
  • Post details in Lost & Found

What happens next

  • Security checks nearby areas
  • Lost item reports help others identify it
  • Police complaint can be filed if required

Food Safety & Hygiene Issues

Use this if you notice unsafe, unhygienic, or repeated food quality issues in mess or tiffin services.

What to do

  • Document the issue clearly (date, meal, problem)
  • Report to hostel/PG management
  • Share feedback through Campus Circle

Why this matters

  • Repeated complaints create accountability
  • Health risks are taken seriously
  • Patterns help prevent future issues
